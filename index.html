01B / EIGHT CENTURIES OF CRAFT

Devotion & Struggle

For eight centuries, the handloom artisans of Nuapatna have woven Khandua Silk as a sacred offering for Odisha's Ratha Yatra, dressing the towering temple chariots of Puri. This thread of faith survived the British colonial era, when machine-made cheap imports flooded the markets and policies restricted local silk production. The weavers' unwavering devotion is the soul that kept the loom alive.

01C / THE ECO LOGIC

Inherently Low Carbon

Our heritage is naturally sustainable. Every saree is crafted on zero-electricity wooden handlooms, colored with organic dyes extracted from local madder roots, turmeric, and indigo. Partnering with BWC Weavers Co-operative, we secure ethical livelihoods.

01D / THE SACRED MYTHOS

Divine Threads

The Calligraphic Weave

In Khandua Patta, verses from poet Jayadeva's 12th-century Sanskrit epic Gita Govinda are woven directly into the body. This unique calligraphic tie-dye process requires absolute structural memory, as the weaver binds individual threads to form sacred shlokas before weaving them.

"Kansari senari..." The silk serves as a medium of prayer, representing a marriage of literature, sacred math, and divine devotion.

The Lord's Daily Attire

Every morning, Lord Jagannath of Puri is adorned with a fresh Khandua silk garment. The colors and motifs change according to the day of the week, aligning with astrological forces.

Red for Sunday, white for Monday, yellow for Wednesday, and black/purple for other days. This daily ritual connects the weaver's loom in Nuapatna directly to the ancient deity sanctum of Puri.

02B / DEVOTION, FOOD & SEA

The Soul of Odisha

Abhada & Stacked Clay Pots

In the sacred temple kitchen of Puri, the Mahaprasad is cooked as a holy offering. In a unique design, 9 clay pots (Kudua) are stacked on top of each other over a single wood fire. Miraculously, the top pot cooks first, then the next, downwards.

This divine culinary physics celebrates simplicity, earthen elements, and centuries of devotion.

Boita Bandana: The Sea Merchant Legacy

During Kartik Purnima, the rivers and shores of Odisha glow with thousands of miniature paper and cork boats (Boita) floating with oil lamps, betel leaves, and coins.

This ritual honors the "Sadhabas" (ancient maritime traders) who sailed across oceans to trade spices and silks, leaving an indelible imprint on Indonesian culture.

The Sacred Posture of Tribhangi

Odissi is the ancient classical dance form of Odisha, characterized by the Tribhangi (the three-bend stance of the head, chest, and pelvis) and complex hand gestures (Mudras).

Carved in stone across temples like Konark and Mukteswar, these poses translate physical geometry into absolute spiritual surrender.
